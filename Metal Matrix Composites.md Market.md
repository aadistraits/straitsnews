<p data-start="726" data-end="747"><strong data-start="726" data-end="747">Market Definition</strong></p>
<p data-start="749" data-end="1240">Metal Matrix Composites (MMCs) are advanced composite materials composed of a metal matrix combined with a reinforcing phase. The matrix is usually an aluminum, titanium, or magnesium alloy, while the reinforcement phase can be a ceramic, polymer, or carbon fiber. MMCs offer a combination of high strength, low weight, and resistance to wear and corrosion, making them highly sought after for high-performance applications in industries like automotive, aerospace, defense, and electronics.</p>
